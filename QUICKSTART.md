This guide will help you create your first soap recipe in minutes.

## Step 1: Set Your Recipe Parameters

In the **Recipe Inputs** section (top-left), configure:

1. **Total Oil Weight**: How much oil you want to use (e.g., 500g)
2. **Unit**: Choose grams (g), ounces (oz), or pounds (lb)
3. **Lye Type**: 
   - NaOH for bar soap
   - KOH for liquid soap
4. **Superfat**: Recommended 5-8% (default is 5%)
5. **Water Method**: Usually "Water as % of Oils" at 38% is fine
6. **Fragrance**: Optional - typically 3-6% of total oil weight

## Step 2: Select Your Oils

In the **Select Oils** section (right side):

1. **Search or browse** the oil database
2. **Click on oils** to add them to your recipe
3. Look for the ‚ú® **sparkle icon** on recommended oils
4. üîí **Locked oils** are incompatible with your current selection

### Pro Tips:
- Start with 2-3 base oils (coconut, palm, olive)
- Add specialty oils for unique properties
- Use the search bar to find oils quickly
- Filter by category (Hard Oil, Soft Oil, Liquid Oil, Butter)

## Step 3: Adjust Oil Percentages

In the **Selected Oils** section (left side):

1. Enter percentages for each oil
2. **Total must equal 100%** (shown at top)
3. Use **"Distribute Equally"** button for even split
4. Remove oils with the ‚ùå button

## Reading Soap Qualities

### Hardness (29-54)
How hard and long-lasting your bar will be
- Too low = mushy soap
- Too high = brittle soap

### Cleansing (12-22)
Deep cleaning power
- Too low = won't clean well
- Too high = drying to skin

### Conditioning (44-69)
Moisturizing properties
- Higher = more moisturizing
- Lower = more drying

### Bubbly (14-46)
Big, fluffy bubbles (short-lived)

### Creamy (16-48)
Stable, long-lasting lather

### Iodine (41-70)
Indicates softness and shelf life
- Lower = harder bar, longer shelf life
- Higher = softer bar, shorter shelf life

### INS (136-165)
Overall quality indicator
- Good balance of all properties
