## Hard Soap vs. Liquid Soap

### Hard (Bar) Soap - NaOH

**Wants:**
- Hardness (29-54) for firm bars
- Balance of all qualities
- Variety of fatty acids

**Highly Recommended:**
- Palm oil (hardness)
- Olive oil (conditioning base)
- Coconut oil (cleansing & bubbles)
- Castor oil (creaminess)

**Incompatible:**
- Very soft oils at high %
- Oils that make bars too soft
- Too much of any one oil

### Liquid Soap - KOH

**Wants:**
- Low hardness (5-15) to stay liquid
- High oleic (>60%) for fluidity
- Low saturated fats (<20%)

**Highly Recommended:**
- Olive oil (high oleic)
- Sunflower oil (stays liquid)
- Safflower oil (low saturates)

**Incompatible:**
- Palm oil (too much palmitic/stearic)
- Cocoa butter (saturated fats)
- Tallow (will solidify)
- Most hard oils/butters

## Understanding Chemistry Terms

**Palmitic/Stearic Acids:**
- Saturated fats
- Make bars hard
- Slow to dissolve
- Not good for liquid soap

**Oleic Acid:**
- Monounsaturated fat
- Conditioning
- Stays liquid
- Perfect for liquid soap

**Lauric/Myristic Acids:**
- Short-chain fats
- Cleansing power
- Creates big bubbles
- Can be drying if too much

**Linoleic/Linolenic Acids:**
- Polyunsaturated fats
- Conditioning
- Can go rancid (DOS)
- Makes bars soft

**Ricinoleic Acid:**
- Unique to castor oil
- Boosts lather
- Creates creaminess
- Use 5-10% only
